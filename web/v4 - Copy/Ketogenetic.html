The Ketogenetic Diet

The diet mainly focuses on macronutrients. It is based on the principle that by depleting the body of carbohydrates , it will burn fat for fuel instead. This is an excellent diet for weight loss because you will gain most of your energy from your own fat stores. If you choose this diet your main meals will mostly be composed of foods that are rich in fats and proteins. The keto diet is generally regarded as a more sustainable weight loss plan as fats and protein are known to increase satiety levels, keeping you feeling full and satisfied for a longer period of time. By following this diet, your body will enter a state called Ketosis.

What is Ketosis?

The goal of the diet is to restrict carbohydrate intake so that the body must turn to fat for fuel. The fat is broken down into ketones that are used as fuel. The ketone bodies are able to reprogram your body, thus making your aging process slower, help you lose fat and stay healthy. Our cells operate better on ketone bodies than on other fuels provided by carbohydrates.

Benefits of the Diet:

The ketogenetic diet has many perks and most importantly, the diet can be sustained for long durations.

Benefits are:

  • Weight loss
  • Reduces risk of certain cancers
  • Improves hearth health
  • Protects brain functions
  • Slows aging
  • Lowers blood pressure
  • Can prevent diabetes

Downsides of the Diet:

Of course, everyone reacts differently to ketosis. These are common symptoms at the beginning of the diet. If they do not lessen or stop, look for an alternative diet.

  • Fatigue
  • Headaches
  • Constipation

This type of eating is not recommended for those who are pregnant or breastfeeding, people with insulin dependent diabetes, kidney disease or pancreatitis.
