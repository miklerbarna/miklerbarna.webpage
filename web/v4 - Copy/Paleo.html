The Paleo Diet

The diet tries to follow an eating habit of the Paleolithic era, which dates from 2.5 million to 10,000 years ago. It includes fish, vegetables, fruits, seeds and lean meats. Basically, it resembles what our hunter-gatherer ancestors ate in that era. Its purpose is to return to a diet that is likely more suitable for the human body. The introduction of farming changed the way people eat. This relatively new and rapid change is said to have outpaced the bodyâ€™s ability to adapt. It is believed that this mismatch is the result of obesity, diabetes and heart disease.

Benefits of the Diet:

For most people, eating more greens will increase the fiber intake, which are important for digestion and a healthy life. The benefits also include:

  • Healthy blood glucose
  • Improved insulin sensitivity
  • Lower blood pressure
  • Weight management
  • Imporved cholesterol balance
  • Improved satiety
  • Improved well-being

Downsides of the Diet:

The main difference between the Paleo and other healthy diets is the absence of grains and legumes. These are considered a good source of fibers, vitamins and nutrients. Dairy products are also something to avoid, which are a good source of protein and calcium. Moreover, buying grass-fed meats may be too expensive for some people.
